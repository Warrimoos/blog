Welcome to the Warrimoo blog — a new home for writing about the things that interest me most: artificial intelligence, software development, veterinary technology, and whatever else seems worth exploring.

## Why a blog?

In a world of tweets and threads, there's still something valuable about long-form writing. A blog post gives you room to think through an idea properly, to show your working, and to come back and update your thinking as you learn more.

I've been building software for a while now, and more recently building [Warrimoo](https://warrimoo.net) and [TreatMyPet](https://warrimoo.net/apps/treatmypet.html). Along the way, I keep running into ideas and observations that deserve more than a passing thought — and this is where they'll live.

## What to expect

This blog will cover a few broad areas:

- **AI and machine learning** — we're living through a remarkable moment in technology. I want to explore what's happening, what it means, and how to think about it clearly.
- **Building software** — lessons from the trenches of shipping real products with a small team.
- **Veterinary technology** — the intersection of pet care and software, and why it matters more than people think.
- **Everything else** — sometimes you just need to write about something.

## Built with Jekyll on GitHub Pages

This blog itself is built with [Jekyll](https://jekyllrb.com/) and hosted on [GitHub Pages](https://pages.github.com/). Posts are written in Markdown, which means I can focus on writing rather than fiddling with a CMS. The design matches the main Warrimoo site — warm coastal colours, clean typography, and hopefully a pleasant reading experience.

If you want to follow along, there's an [RSS feed](/feed.xml) for those of us who still appreciate that sort of thing.

See you in the next post.
